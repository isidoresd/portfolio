01 Background

17/01/21

According to the National Health Service, there are 1.2 million wheelchair users in the UK alone, a striking 1.8% of the population.The International Spinal Cord Society estimates that as of 2019, at least 1200 new people suffer from some kind of spinal cord injuries every year with 35% of these causing teraplegia, a severe form of paralysis.

Tetraplegia (often reffered to as quadriplegia) is caused by damage to the spinal cord or brain known as lesions. Lesions can be the result of trauma, such as injury, diseases such as multiple sclerosis, or congenital disorders such as muscular dystrophy. The large majority of cases being caused by trauma according to the National Spinal Cord Injury Statistcal Center. Spinal injuries are categorised determined by where the spinal cord is damaged, the further up the spine the damage occurs, the higher the severity of the paralysis.
