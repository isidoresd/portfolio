16 Pressure Sensing Introduction

30/03/21

This post will develop some of my ideas for pressure ulcer risk reduction, namely automatic pressure sensing and chair actuation. If the chair detects prolonged pressure then it can take the neccessary steps to relieve that pressure.
Firstly, I will examine the pressure sensing capabilities. I will use Tinkercad.com which has a simulated Arduino Uno, a popular microcontroller.

The circuit depicted consists of a piezo buzzer and force sensor connected to an Arduino. The idea is for the force applied to exceed a predetermined amount, in this instance 0.5 N, which then actuates the piezo. The piezo is used here as a standin for possibly a stepper-motor or hydaulic piston that tilts the chair.

The code to the right is written in Arduino/C++. I had run two other experiments with no success as of yet. These were an averaging of applied force over a period of time to then actuate the chair if average pressure exceeds a threshold and actuating a piezo for just a specific time period to represent the chair tilting a small degree each incidence of detrimental pressure. My knowledge of programming is somewhat limited currently. I wish to develop these ideas further later.
