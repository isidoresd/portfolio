07 Pressure Ulcers

04/02/21

After researching problems with existing wheelchair solutions, one in particular would often appear. Pressure ulcers, often called pressure sores. Defined by the National Health Service as injuries to skin and underlying tissue caused by prolonged pressure on the skin. Although anyone can be afflicted, people who are immobile, such as those with tetraplegia, are especially at risk.

Pressure ulcers if allowed to develop, can be extremely painful and cause secondary conditions such as blood poisining. It is therefore neccessary to consider how to counter pressure ulcers when someone is immobile. Often the help of a caregiver is required to periodically adjust the patients position to relieve pressure. There are designs that help with this problem. Research of existing products can show how designers are already seeking to alleviate this problem.

One method currently in use are pressure relief cushions. They utilise two main methods: gel and air. Air ones provide the possibility of dynamically relieving pressure as and when is neccessary. As discussed in my previous supervisory meeting, it could even be possible to automate this function using pressure sensors and an arduino. Another more advanced option could utilise a raspberry pi, phone and pressure sensors to allow a patient or caregiver to view a pressure map and make adjustments to the chairs settings from within a phone application.

Sitting posture or sitting position also has an effect on the likelihood of pressure ulcer development.
